DEPRESSION

Keep yourself busy, and there will be no time for depression.

Depression — అవసాదం — अवसाद

A word that sounds small when spoken, but feels unbearably heavy when carried.
“Once upon a time… No… wait.
Let’s start in a different way.
Generally, people get depressed when they lose someone they love… when death steals a person they were not ready to let go of. Some fall into it after betrayal from the people they trusted the most. Some after broken relationships. Some after losing their dignity, reputation, dreams, or facing the biggest failure of their lives.
Life suddenly stops.
The world keeps moving, but somewhere inside, they don’t.
The truth is — life asks us to start fresh, to begin again, to rebuild ourselves from the ruins.
But how?

How do you move on when your heart still waits for someone who is gone?
How do you imagine life without them?
How do you walk forward when your feet are stuck in yesterday?
How do you face reality when reality itself hurts?

And then the memories come.
The places.
The favourites.
The late-night discussions.
The laughs.
The pain.
The support.
The moments they stood beside you.
The happiest memories suddenly become the heaviest wounds.
Every small thing starts hitting hard.
And depression slowly whispers: “Stay here. Don’t move.”
But life whispers something else: “You still have a chance.”
Men or women — strength is not about never crying.
Strength is crying, breaking, feeling lost… and still choosing to stand up.
Yes, it is hard…. Very hard….. I understand.
Because I have lived through it too.
I got depressed when I was broken.
I got depressed when I failed to get a job.
I got depressed when I felt I had lost my family’s trust in me.
There were nights I cried, Days when I felt helpless, Moments when I felt completely alone.
No one beside me, No one to understand me. And then one day,
I asked myself: “Why can’t I be with myself when I am alone?”
Maybe no one stood by my side. But I stood by mine.
I believed in myself.
I trusted myself.
And slowly, I realised: I am not alone. I have me.
So, I changed.
I changed my thinking.
I changed my way of speaking.
I changed my attitude.
I changed my behaviour.
Not because the world asked me to, But because I wanted to be happy.
And I promised myself: “I will be happy.”
This is not the end.
This pain is not your final chapter.
This heartbreak, This failure, This loneliness, This darkness.
Maybe… Just maybe… It is your second chance to restart life.
A new version of you is waiting on the other side of pain.
So cry if you need to, Feel sad if you must, Take your time.
But don’t stop living.
Keep moving.
Keep growing.
Keep trying.
And most importantly — Keep yourself busy, because depression feeds on empty silence.

Depression — Keep yourself busy, and one day, you won’t have time for it anymore.
